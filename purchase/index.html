License

One license is eligible for a single app bundle ID and Enterprise App Store. Additional licenses must be purchased for additional apps/deployments.

Applications that expose PSPDFKit's functionality via a login system that is bound to a backend service where customers can pay for access require a custom license agreement. (See our license for details.)

If you're unsure about your license requirements or have a different use case, contact us with your project requirements.
All prices are excluding VAT, if you are in a country that requires VAT.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an explicit application ID?

An explicit application ID is a string identifier unique to each iOS app. Each PSPDFKit license is tied to such an identifier.

Do you offer discounts for educational and non-profit organizations?

Yes, we love to support projects in the educational and non-profit space. Contact us with your project requirements using your .edu or non-profit organization email.

Do you provide support?

Of course! We can help you with all aspects on integrating PSPDFKit and we're open for feedback/bugs/feature requests to further improve our product. Please always contact us with your PET* purchase number so we can help you more efficiently. This is especially important for Enterprise customers with Priority Support, as those emails will be on top of the queue and we'll extend our answers with example code when needed. We sometimes also implement smaller features on the fly for Enterprise customers. You can also contact support if you're still evaluating.

Do you require some form of revenue sharing?

Usually the license is a one-time payment and includes support for 12 months. After that, you will have the opportunity to extend your support and update period. Certain situations, such as having one app with a paid backend service, might require a custom contract with revenue sharing or a charge per user/device. Please contact us with details about your product.

Do you have a user limit per license?

Our license is limited to the application ID and enterprise deplyment variant. For simply apps, there is no user limitation. If your application requires a login for your service, you need to contact us for a custom license.

Does your SDK work with the iPhone/iPod Touch?

Yes, PSPDFKit has several special improvements so you can efficiently work with documents even on the smaller screen of an iPhone or iPod Touch.

What features are enabled in PSPDFKit Viewer?

PSPDFKit Viewer is our entry-level product and perfect for magazine-like applications that don't require any of the more advanced features like strong encryption, annotation editing, text selection, index search, or PDF forms. Viewer has all basic features like PDF search, multimedia annotation support (videos, audio, and web pages, including custom UIViews) and all page transitions like per-page scrolling, continuous scroll, and page curl.

Can we upgrade later on?

Yes, you can update to more feature-rich options by paying the price difference and a small update fee. Keep in mind that this doesn't extend your one-year updates cycle. Contact us with your PET* purchase number for details.

Our company policy forbids us from being listed as a customer.

The license terms for PSPDFKit Enterprise are negotiable. Contact us for details.

What is the upgrade price after the first year?

All options include 12 months of updates and support. If you extend your upgrade time within that period for another 12 months, it's 50% of the then-current price of a license. If you decide to upgrade after your update period has ended, we'll charge 75% of the price of a new license. You'll see the status of each license in our customer portal, so it's easy to keep track and extend them as needed. (Note: PSPDFKit Viewer previously had a six-month update cycle, but this was changed in January 2014.)

Can I use PSPDFKit with Enterprise apps?

Yes. The same explicit app ID restrictions apply. If you're using PSPDFKit and need multiple licenses, contact us with your requirements.

We sell our solution to different companies via the Enterprise App Store. Do we need multiple licenses?

Yes. Even if you use the same application ID, if you distribute the app in multiple Enterprise App Stores and/or apply different branding, you need to purchase one license per branded application. We're flexible with such use cases, but it's best to contact us for a custom agreement.

We're offering a lite version and/or different versions for iPad and iPhone. Will we need multiple licenses?

We have exceptions for certain use cases. Having a lite/full version is one of them — you're fine with one license. The same goes for the same application with two different versions for iPhone and iPad.

Will we need a separate license for beta versions / internal client demos?

No. You can use the license you already have to distribute beta versions, even if the beta uses a different bundle ID. Client demos are also fine, as long as they're really just a demo and not the finished product.

Can you clarify what "Binary distribution is allowed within your app as long as it's statically linked in one binary" means?

This is just to ensure that you're not simply giving away the framework for free to download. If you integrate it into your app, Xcode will automatically statically link that to your final app binary.

What's the Appcelerator Titanium Module?

We offer a JavaScript Bridge that works with Appcelerator Titanium. Appcelerator is a technology that allows rapid application development for companies that are more experienced with JavaScript than Objective-C.

What all is supported in the Appcelerator Titanium bridge?

PSPDFKit exposes only a subset of the features in the Titanium port — enough to make a great app, probably not enough if your app requires heavy customization. The Titanium code is open source, so you can customize and extend the plugin bridge.

We are using Xamarin (MonoTouch) / RubyMotion / Cordova (PhoneGap). Are you supporting this?

Yes. We closely work with Xamarin to create up-to-date bindings. They might take a bit longer to update than the Objective-C releases, but we're committed to supporting them. The Apache Cordova bindings are open source, and you can get the Appcelerator Titanium bindings via an email request. For RubyMotion, you can simply use the existing API. See this blog post for a tutorial.
